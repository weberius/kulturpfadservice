The Cologne patrician family Scherffgen was in the 16th and 17th. Century in Cologne very influential and was one of the richest families in the city. They traded in goods such as wine, cereals and spices and were also active in banking. The Scherffgens were members of the Cologne Council and held various offices, including that of the mayor. The family owned several magnificent city palaces and became known for its generous foundations and charity work. A well-known member of the family was Johann Peter Scherffgen, who in the 18th Century as a lawyer and writer. Today, a street in Cologne still commemorates the once influential family.
